## Relevance to Keyridge Mandates

### Multi-Asset Funds -- POSITIVE SIGNAL
- Multi-asset/mixed investment funds are the standout positive category in 2026
- February: +GBP 1.5bn inflows; March: +GBP 1.3bn inflows; April: continued positive
- Investors are rotating INTO multi-asset as a diversified safe-haven during geopolitical volatility
- Multi-asset flows are anchored by monthly savings plans, providing stability
- **Implication for Keyridge:** Strong tailwind. IFAs are directing client money into multi-asset strategies. Keyridge multi-asset propositions should be well-positioned on platforms.

### UK Equity Funds -- NEGATIVE SIGNAL (HEADWIND)
- UK equity is the worst-performing category: record outflows of -GBP 836m in April 2026
- 10+ consecutive months of outflows extending back to mid-2025
- Of GBP 49bn invested in equity funds since 2015, zero net new money has gone to UK-focused funds
- **Implication for Keyridge:** Significant headwind for UK equity mandates. IFAs are actively de-allocating. Keyridge needs a strong differentiation story (value, income, contrarian) to swim against this tide. Consider positioning UK equity as a contrarian opportunity.

### Fixed Income / Bond Funds -- VOLATILE BUT RECOVERING
- March saw heavy outflows (-GBP 535m) on oil-shock inflation / yield spike fears
- April saw strong recovery (+GBP 610m) as investors sought safer assets
- Bond funds are volatile month-to-month but remain in overall demand
- **Implication for Keyridge:** Bond fund mandates face short-term volatility but medium-term tailwinds as investors seek income and safety. Rate sensitivity messaging matters -- IFAs need clarity on duration positioning.

### Active vs Passive Shift
- March saw -GBP 2.4bn from active strategies vs +GBP 1bn into passive
- This ongoing structural shift is a headwind for ALL active managers including Keyridge
- **Implication for Keyridge:** Must demonstrate clear active management value-add to justify fees. IFA conversations should focus on outcomes, risk-adjusted returns, and downside protection.
