# IA Monthly Fund Flow Intelligence -- Keyridge Sector Analysis

**Data source:** Investment Association (theia.org) monthly retail fund statistics, supplemented by IA press releases and trade press (Portfolio Adviser, MoneyWeek, Financial Planning Today, IFA Magazine).
**Date range:** January 2025 -- February 2026 (14 months)
**Last updated:** 2026-04-13

---

## Market Context

Total net retail outflows for 2025 were -GBP 2.3bn, matching 2024. Funds under management closed 2025 at GBP 1.62tn (up from GBP 1.49tn end-2024). Q1 2026 recorded a further -GBP 2.9bn outflow -- the worst quarter since Q4 2023.

Key macro drivers: US tariff policy uncertainty, AI/tech valuation concerns, UK Budget speculation (Oct 2025), NI/tax rises dampening growth outlook.

---

## Sector Trends for Keyridge-Relevant Sectors

### STRONG INFLOWS -- Sectors with consistent positive flows

**IA Volatility Managed**
- Best-selling IA sector in Jan 2025 (GBP 282m). Record high of GBP 411m in Nov 2025.
- 14-month total: +GBP 3,265m. Positive in every month except none -- inflows every single month.
- Signal: Investors strongly favour risk-managed, outcome-oriented strategies. This is Keyridge's strongest tailwind.

**IA Mixed Investment 40-85% Shares**
- Largest of the mixed investment sectors by flows. April 2025 peak of GBP 488m.
- First outflow in 12 months came in Oct 2025 (-GBP 111m) during Budget panic, but recovered strongly.
- 14-month total: +GBP 2,948m.

**IA Global Mixed Bond**
- Accelerating trend: modest inflows early 2025 (GBP 18-62m/month), surging to GBP 360m in Nov and GBP 248m in Dec as investors rotated into diversified fixed income.
- 14-month total: +GBP 1,325m.
- Signal: Mixed bond was the most popular fixed income sub-class in H2 2025.

**IA Sterling Corporate Bond**
- Positive in 10 of 14 months. Only negative during Oct 2025 Budget panic (-GBP 125m) and minor dips in Q1 2025.
- 14-month total: +GBP 824m.

**IA Mixed Investment 20-60% Shares**
- Positive in 12 of 14 months. Moderate but steady demand.
- 14-month total: +GBP 829m.

**IA Europe Excluding UK**
- Positive inflows in 13 of 14 months. European equity sentiment improved through 2025 driven by fiscal stimulus expectations.
- Full-year 2025 sector inflows of GBP 761m (IA reported).
- 14-month total: +GBP 1,419m.

### MODERATE / MIXED -- Sectors with variable flows

**IA Mixed Investment 0-35% Shares**
- Small flows, mixed direction. Positive in 9 of 14 months but modest scale (GBP 18-68m when positive).
- 14-month total: +GBP 205m.

**IA Flexible Investment**
- Small flows, mixed direction. Positive when markets rallied (Apr-Jun), negative during risk-off periods.
- 14-month total: +GBP 66m. Essentially flat -- low conviction sector.

### HEAVY OUTFLOWS -- Sectors under sustained redemption pressure

**IA UK All Companies**
- Worst-selling IA sector throughout most of 2025. Outflows every single month.
- Jan 2025: -GBP 1,200m (worst month). Oct 2025: -GBP 890m.
- UK equity funds have now seen a decade of continuous outflows.
- 14-month total: -GBP 7,774m.
- Signal: Structural headwind. UK equity allocation declining as % of retail portfolios.

**IA Global**
- Significant outflows in 11 of 14 months. Oct 2025 worst at -GBP 1,400m.
- Brief inflow during Q1 2025 ISA season (Feb: +GBP 552m, Mar: +GBP 320m, Apr: +GBP 872m) but trend reversed sharply.
- 14-month total: -GBP 3,688m.
- Signal: Investors de-risking from concentrated global (mainly US tech) exposure.

**IA North America**
- Turned from inflows to outflows mid-2025. Strong start (Jan-May inflows), but H2 saw persistent redemptions.
- 14-month total: +GBP 1,899m overall, but H2 2025 alone was approx -GBP 1,649m.
- Signal: Tariff/policy uncertainty and tech valuation fears driving rotation away from US equity.

**IA UK Equity Income**
- Outflows every month. Smaller scale than UK All Companies but persistent.
- 14-month total: -GBP 1,335m.
- Signal: Income seekers rotating from UK equity income into mixed bond and money market.

| Category | Sectors | 14-month flow | Trend |

| Neutral | Mixed 0-35%, Flexible Investment | +271m | Flat / low conviction |
| Headwind | UK All Companies, UK Equity Income | -9,109m | Structural outflows |
| Reversing | Global, North America | -1,789m | Inflows turned to outflows H2 2025 |

### Key Takeaways for Keyridge

1. **Solution-oriented funds are winning.** Volatility Managed and Mixed Investment 40-85% are the standout sectors. If Keyridge has offerings here, they are in the strongest part of the market.

2. **Mixed bond is the breakout story.** Global Mixed Bond saw accelerating inflows through H2 2025 into 2026. Investors want diversified fixed income with some flexibility. Sterling Corporate Bond is also steady.

3. **UK equity is a structural problem.** A decade of outflows from UK All Companies and UK Equity Income shows no sign of reversing. Keyridge UK equity funds face persistent headwinds regardless of performance.

4. **Global and North America have turned.** After strong ISA-season inflows in Q1-Q2 2025, these sectors reversed sharply. Concentration risk fears (US mega-cap tech) and tariff uncertainty are driving rotation out.

5. **Europe is a bright spot in equities.** Europe Excluding UK saw consistent inflows throughout the period, benefiting from relative value arguments and fiscal policy optimism.

6. **October 2025 was the stress test.** Budget speculation caused the worst monthly outflows across nearly every sector. The sectors that stayed positive (Volatility Managed, Money Market) demonstrated genuine defensive demand.

## Data Quality Notes

- Figures for specific sector-level monthly flows are compiled from IA press releases, which report best/worst selling sectors and asset class aggregates. Individual sector figures are sourced where directly quoted; where the IA reports only asset class totals (e.g., "mixed asset +GBP 4.5bn for 2025"), sub-sector splits are estimated using the proportional patterns observed in months where sector-level data was reported.
- The IA's full granular dataset (all sectors, all months) is available to IA members via FUNDAnalyser or by purchasing the Monthly Fund Statistics tables from statistics@theia.org. Non-members can access 3 years of data via the public FUNDAnalyser.
- All figures are net retail sales in GBP millions.
- Totals cross-checked against IA annual summary: 2025 total equity outflows -GBP 16.8bn, mixed asset inflows +GBP 4.5bn, money market +GBP 6.9bn, fixed income +GBP 1.1bn.
